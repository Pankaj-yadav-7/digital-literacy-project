### General Digital Safety
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Use apps like Google Authenticator or hardware keys for your University email, WhatsApp, and Instagram. Avoid SMS-based 2FA if possible.
- [ ] **Vet App Permissions:** Before installing third-party productivity or gaming apps, check if they are asking for unnecessary access to your contacts, microphone, or SMS.
- [ ] **Secure Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into student portals or banking apps while on campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection.
- [ ] **Practice Link Skepticism:** Do not click on links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming your Electricity Bill is overdue, KYC is expired, or offering Free Internship Certifications.
- [ ] **Lock Your SIM Card:** Set up a SIM PIN in your phone settings. This prevents scammers from using your SIM in another device to intercept OTPs if your phone is stolen.
- [ ] **Update Software Regularly:** Ensure your OS and browser (Chrome/Edge) are updated to patch vulnerabilities that hackers use to inject malware.

### Financial & UPI Safety
- [ ] **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Remember that you **never** need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money. If someone asks you to do this, it is a scam.
- [ ] **Verify Merchant Names:** When scanning a QR code at a local shop or for a college fest, always double-check the "Verified Merchant" name displayed on the screen before hitting 'Pay'.

### How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know falls victim to a scam, act immediately:
1.  **Call the National Helpline:** Dial **1930** (24/7 National Cybercrime Reporting Toll-free Number).
2.  **File an Online Complaint:** Visit the official portal at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)**.
3.  **Report to Bank:** If it is a financial fraud, notify your bank’s cyber cell within 2 hours (the "Golden Hour") to increase the chances of freezing the transaction.
